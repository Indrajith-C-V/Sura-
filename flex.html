Flex

In physical action and slang To bend or contract: The verb "flex" can mean to bend a joint or tense a muscle. An athlete might flex their muscles to show their strength. To boast or show off: In slang, "a flex" is an obvious display of wealth, possessions, or achievements, often done to impress or intimidate others. In technology and software Flex (software framework): An open-source application framework used to build and maintain cross-platform web, desktop, and mobile applications. Developers use the Flex SDK to create interactive, data-driven applications that can run consistently on iOS, Android, and web browsers using Adobe AIR and the Flash Player. Flex (lexical analyzer generator): A free software tool that generates lexical analyzers (or "scanners") that recognize lexical patterns in text. It is often used with a parser generator like GNU Bison to create compilers and interpreters. CSS flexbox: In web development, "flex" refers to the flexible box layout module, a one-dimensional layout method used to arrange items in a container. In electrical engineering and construction Electrical cable: In British English, "flex" is an insulated electric cable that contains multiple wires and is used to connect an appliance to a power source. Flexible Flat Cable (FFC): A type of very thin, flat electrical cable with multiple metallic conductors bonded to a plastic film base. FFCs are common in high-density electronics like laptops, cell phones, and printers. Flexible materials: In construction, a "flex" material is any flexible material designed to bend or conform without losing structural integrity. Examples include flexible concrete, bendable plywood, and various plastic membranes for roofing. Flex banner: A type of PVC (polyvinyl chloride) banner material that is durable, lightweight, and used for outdoor digital printing. In other contexts Flex football: A limited-contact football game that focuses on technique, teamwork, and sportsmanship.
